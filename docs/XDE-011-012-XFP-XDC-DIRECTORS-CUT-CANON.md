# XDE Director's Cut and Finish Canon

**Status:** Canonical upon merge
**Authority:** Darren Cureton
**Release:** Executive Documentary Director's Cut v1.0

## XDE-011 — Narrative Continuity Engine

Every scene must resolve the prior question, explain why the answer matters, reveal the next idea, and invite the next question. No scene may introduce a subject without a cause-and-effect bridge from the preceding scene.

## XDE-012 — Single Stream Principle

The documentary operates as one uninterrupted executive conversation. Scene boundaries may change composition, but may not reset the subject, tone, or logic. Every scene must feel like the next sentence in the same thought.

## XFP-001 — Xen Finishing Protocol

After story architecture is approved, production enters Finish Mode. All script, visual, motion, timing, sound, trust, polish, documentary-flow, and executive-simulation passes must be completed before release certification.

## XFP-002 — Executive Director's Cut

After Picture Lock, no new architecture, controller, runtime system, scene, or feature may be added. Only script, pacing, typography, spacing, transition, animation, narration, interaction polish, accessibility, performance, and verified defect correction are permitted.

## XDC-001 — Executive Director's Cut Authority

The Director's Cut is the highest creative authority for the release. No line or visual survives because it was difficult to build. Every element must advance comprehension, emotion, ownership, or mandate.

## XDC-002 — Respect the Executive

Every second must respect the executive's time, intelligence, and attention. Never over-explain, oversell, animate for decoration, or repeat an understood point. The experience lets the executive reach the conclusion.

## Locked narrative stream

1. Growth creates knowledge.
2. Growth risks losing what it teaches.
3. Documentation is the traditional response.
4. Documentation captures information but rarely preserves judgment.
5. The BDC proves a living operating standard.
6. Leadership teaches the company.
7. Approved judgment becomes operating memory.
8. One company retains what works; the group compounds it.
9. The executive chooses where proof begins.
10. The selected branch becomes the next controlled build.

## Locked narration

Narration is concise, visible, scene-bound, and never blocks interaction. It remains coherent without audio. A future recorded voice may read the locked lines verbatim but may not change their meaning without reopening Picture Lock.

## Production freeze

The release is feature-frozen when all repository gates pass and the branch is merged to main. Further changes require either a verified defect or explicit authorization to reopen production.
