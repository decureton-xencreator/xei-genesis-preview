# XEI-001 — EXECUTIVE PREMIERE CINEMATIC STORYBOARD V5

Status: Production Storyboard
Audience: Edward, Owner — Checkmate Holding Group
Runtime Target: 12–15 minutes guided / 20–30 minutes interactive
Format: Scroll-directed cinematic web film with guided XVS narration
Purpose: Make Ed experience the BDC Operating System as proof that Xen can give every Checkmate company memory, direction, and living operating intelligence.

## Governing Idea

This is not a presentation and not a dashboard tour. It is a directed executive film that Ed enters, influences, and changes.

The emotional progression is:

Curiosity → Recognition → Tension → Proof → Participation → Transformation → Scale → Desire → Mandate

The BDC system is the proof. Xen is the reveal. Ed's enterprise is the opportunity.

## Direction Rules

1. Xen guides Ed continuously. No dead screens and no unexplained controls.
2. Each act has a clear camera intention: establish, push in, orbit, descend, reveal, pull back.
3. The experience scrolls automatically during narrated sequences and pauses only when Ed must act.
4. Text appears in timed phrases rather than all at once.
5. Graphics move with narrative meaning. Motion never exists only to decorate.
6. The diamond cursor is Xen's living presence. It leads attention, blinks while thinking, and expands when Xen speaks.
7. Voice and visual timing share one scene clock.
8. Every claim is truth-labelled as real, demonstrated, simulated, or proposed.
9. The final executive summary is generated from Ed's answers and contribution.
10. The closing question must create the second meeting without asking Ed to buy software.

# CAMERA AND MOTION ARCHITECTURE

## Camera vocabulary

- Establishing wide: Introduce company-scale concepts.
- Slow push-in: Create intimacy before important questions.
- Orbit: Show living relationships and dimensionality.
- Vertical descent: Move from enterprise concept into operational proof.
- Macro close-up: Highlight Ed's words, approval, and evidence.
- Rapid pullback: Reveal that BDC is part of something larger.
- Constellation pullback: Reveal all twenty-four companies.
- Corridor dolly: Turn the close into a choice and mandate.

## Scroll choreography

The browser scroll is part of the direction. Each scene consists of scroll chapters with fixed visual layers and moving narrative content.

- Xen narration triggers scroll cues.
- Each cue moves to a named anchor.
- Scroll duration is calculated from narration length.
- User wheel/touch pauses auto-direction for five seconds.
- Xen resumes only after displaying a subtle “Continue with Xen” prompt.
- Interactive moments lock the cinematic timeline until Ed responds.

## Timing system

Each narration segment contains:

- voice start
- visual entrance offset
- camera transform
- highlight target
- scroll anchor
- pause duration
- interaction gate
- transition cue

No scene advances based only on an arbitrary timer. It advances when narration, motion, and required interaction have all completed.

# XVS VOICE DIRECTOR REQUIREMENTS

Browser speech synthesis alone is not sufficient for the Gold Master premiere because voice availability is inconsistent and may select an unacceptable voice.

Production requirement:

1. Use a pre-rendered approved British female XVS narration track for every fixed line.
2. Browser speech synthesis may be used only for dynamic content such as repeating Ed's contribution.
3. Dynamic synthesis must be disabled if an approved British female voice is unavailable.
4. The experience must never silently substitute a male voice.
5. If the approved voice track fails to load, display captions and a clear “Narration unavailable” state rather than using a wrong voice.
6. Captions are always available and synchronised to narration.

# VISUAL DESIGN DIRECTION

The standard is feature-film title design translated into executive software.

- Deep black and dimensional charcoal rather than flat dark grey.
- Controlled emerald light as Xen's presence.
- Platinum and warm white for executive content.
- No generic card-wall dashboards.
- Large-scale typography with disciplined negative space.
- Volumetric light, depth blur, parallax, reflections, and restrained particles.
- Graphic transitions must express transformation: fragments becoming memory, pages becoming infrastructure, one manual becoming an orchestra, one company becoming twenty-four.
- Every major act receives a distinct visual world while retaining one design language.
