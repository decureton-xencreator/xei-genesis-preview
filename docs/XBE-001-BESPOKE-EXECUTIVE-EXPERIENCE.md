# XBE-001 · Bespoke Executive Experience

## Executive outcome

The private premiere is an executive journey, not a file drop. It begins with a recipient-specific Checkmate arrival, gives Xen a clearly governed role, reveals proof progressively, records the viewer's decision, and ends only when the viewer deliberately continues to a second appointment.

## Experience sequence

1. **Private arrival** — full Checkmate Holding Group lockup, recipient name, confidentiality cue, one entry action.
2. **Xen introduction** — smaller Checkmate seal; “Hello Ed,” “Hello Kim,” or “Hello Ahmer”; Xen explains why she is present.
3. **Proof, not claims** — the BDC operating system and complete governed manuals provide inspectable evidence.
4. **Executive participation** — the viewer teaches Xen one principle and chooses one proof path.
5. **Path-faithful close** — the finale reflects the viewer's choice and does not trigger the second-appointment narration until the continuation button is selected.
6. **Closed-loop follow-up** — private choice reporting returns starts, selections, completions, and continuation intent to the owner.

## Logo placement authority

| Surface | Placement | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Private arrival | Large, centered full lockup | Establishes ownership and value before Xen appears |
| Xen introduction | Small centered seal above the salutation | Transfers authority from Checkmate to the guided experience |
| Documentary header | 18px co-brand glyph before XEN | Maintains provenance without competing with content |
| Finale and appointment handoff | Co-brand treatment in the close | Reconnects the proof to the commercial next step |
| Publication cover | Full lockup, top third | Owns the document immediately |
| Publication interior | Small glyph in running header | Navigation and provenance only |
| Publication close | Full co-brand lockup | Marks the call to action and second appointment |

The logo is never placed behind body copy, used as a low-contrast obstacle, or allowed to cross a heading.

## Publication integrity

- Every heading stays with the first paragraph or visual block it introduces.
- Cards, diagrams, tables, signatures, and callouts are atomic blocks and never split across pages.
- A new section begins on the next page when less than one complete block remains.
- The cover, executive premise, proof architecture, recommendation, and call to action each receive a complete page composition.
- Running headers are subordinate; no border, shimmer, watermark, or decorative line may pass through language.
- Final PDFs require rendered page-by-page visual inspection before release.

- Apple event experiences use a cinematic hero, one primary action, and progressive disclosure rather than exposing the entire product story at once. The arrival gate applies that discipline to the premiere. <https://www.apple.com/apple-events/>
- McKinsey's journey work emphasizes seamless, secure, omnichannel, on-demand, personalized experiences. The premiere preserves one storyline across phone, tablet, and desktop and personalizes by recipient. <https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/growth-marketing-and-sales/how-we-help-clients/journey-analytics>
- IBM Innovation Studio frames executive work as explore, co-create, and leave with a plan. The documentary moves from possibility to participation to a path-specific second appointment. <https://www.ibm.com/about/innovation-studio/stockholm>
- McKinsey's personalization guidance supports proving value through a focused pilot before scaling. Xen presents the BDC as the proof and asks the executive to choose the next controlled proof. <https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/tech-and-ai/how-we-help-clients/personalization-transforms-customer-experience>
- Journey-based management and closed-loop follow-up support recording the viewer's chosen path and continuation intent rather than treating the premiere as a passive video. <https://www.mckinsey.com/about-us/overview/alliances/medallia-and-mckinsey>

## XMP Milestone 4 presentation inheritance

The executive kit inherits the verified XMP Milestone 4 shell principles without pretending to be a music or provider interface. The premiere uses one `100dvh` presentation shell, a dominant bordered stage, a compact persistent dock, desktop two-plane composition where the scene supports it, and stage-first tablet/mobile priorities.

- **Content before chrome:** the story remains the primary surface; navigation stays compact.
- **Truthful controls:** only previous, play/pause, next, and restart are exposed because those are the premiere capabilities actually implemented.
- **Responsive priority:** desktop uses the wider executive stage; tablet collapses spacing first; mobile preserves the locked scene geometry and safe-area dock.
- **Accessibility:** essential actions retain visible keyboard focus, labels, state announcements, and reduced-motion equivalents.
- **Performance:** ambient effects remain transform/opacity based; the inheritance adds no provider SDK, background network dependency, or layout-driven animation.
