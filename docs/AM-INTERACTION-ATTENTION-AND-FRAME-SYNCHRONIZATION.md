# AM — Interaction Attention & Frame Synchronization

Status: Active / Xen-wide
Origin: Executive Premiere live validation, 2026-07-15

## Permanent Rule

Every narrated or guided Xen experience must preserve the complete visual sentence while narration is delivered. Camera movement, scrolling, captions, imagery, and spoken words must share one intended frame and must never cause headings, critical copy, graphics, or controls to be cut off.

Whenever the user must act, the required interaction becomes the dominant visual object. Choices, text fields, approvals, and mandate controls must illuminate, pulse with restraint, enter the safe viewport, receive an explicit `YOUR TURN` cue, and remain visually dominant until the action is completed.

## Required Sequence

1. Frame the complete scene beneath persistent chrome.
2. Deliver narration while preserving the scene's establishing composition.
3. Move only when a narrative beat requires movement.
4. Complete the final spoken beat.
5. Reframe to the required interaction.
6. Illuminate the interaction and pause all auto-advancement.
7. Resume only after explicit user action.

## Prohibited Patterns

- Scroll-to-centre on every spoken line.
- Camera motion that clips the scene heading or primary graphic.
- Interaction controls appearing below the fold without a guided reframe.
- Simultaneous narration and unsignalled interaction.
- Auto-advance while the user is expected to answer.
- Decorative motion that competes with the next required action.

## Inheritance

This modification applies to Executive Symphony, Living Manuals, XEI Director Mode, Diamond Mobile experiences, Academy, simulations, executive assessments, and all future guided Xen runtimes.
