# XEI Executive Experience Review Board

## Status
Active governing review standard for all future XEI executive demonstrations.

## Canon break
The label **Executive Documentary Engine** is retired and prohibited. The experience is not governed as a documentary, slideshow, dashboard tour, or software walkthrough.

The approved product category is:

**Cinematic Executive Experience**

Its job is to let an executive understand, influence, and remember a strategic operating idea through a reliable interactive experience.

## Foundation protected
The clean phone runtime discovered in Executive Premiere V1 is the permanent reliability foundation:

- one production page
- one JavaScript owner
- direct synchronous entry
- manual forward and back navigation
- no required speech engine
- no hidden activation controls
- no parallel scene directors
- no floating captions
- no delayed start handoff
- no runtime patch stacking

Cinematic improvement must occur through content, composition, CSS motion, deterministic interaction, and carefully bounded state—not through additional controllers.

## Review board seats
Every release is reviewed through these perspectives:

1. **Executive Strategist** — Does the experience create the desired business conclusion without exaggeration?
2. **Story Architect** — Does each scene create curiosity, recognition, proof, participation, scale, and a natural next decision?
3. **Apple Product Editor** — Is every word, control, transition, and visual necessary, refined, and obvious?
4. **Netflix Experience Director** — Does tension build, do reveals land, and is there an emotional peak and memorable close?
5. **Enterprise Operator** — Are the operating claims credible, measurable, and connected to real Checkmate conditions?
6. **Interaction Reliability Lead** — Can the complete experience be operated on the target phone without hidden dependencies or ambiguous controls?
7. **Truth and Governance Lead** — Are real, demonstrated, proposed, and future capabilities clearly distinguished?

## Release questions
A candidate may proceed only when the board can answer yes to all of the following:

- Does the first screen work with one tap?
- Can the complete experience be understood without voice?
- Does every scene contain one dominant idea and one visual memory?
- Do button labels advance the story rather than merely say Continue?
- Is there one unmistakable transformation moment?
- Does executive participation visibly change a later scene?
- Does the ending create a next meeting without a conventional sales pitch?
- Are all animations decorative-safe and non-blocking?
- Does reduced-motion mode preserve the complete experience?
- Is there exactly one runtime owner?

## Approved reusable cinematic patterns
Low-risk patterns that may be inherited:

- orbiting diamond presence built entirely in CSS
- floating knowledge fragments
- dimensional manual family reveal
- ask → understand → act progression
- executive principle selection
- visible propagation from executive judgment to connected operating layers
- enterprise constellation around executive purpose
- scene-specific action labels
- final proof statement and controlled mandate

## Prohibited regression patterns

- Executive Documentary Engine terminology
- auto-playing narration as a dependency
- multiple JavaScript controllers
- automatic scene advancement
- hidden desktop controls on phone
- duplicated transport or caption systems
- animation-frame start gates
- browser-voice detection before entry
- visual density that forces emergency scrolling to reach required controls
- certification suites tied to retired architectures

## Current experience standard
Executive Premiere V2 preserves the reliable V1 foundation and adds cinematic depth through nine scenes, CSS motion, dynamic story labels, the **You taught the company** propagation reveal, an enterprise constellation, and a stronger proof-based ending.
